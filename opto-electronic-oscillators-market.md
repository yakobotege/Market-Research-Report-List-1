<p><p>The opto-electronic oscillators (OEO) market comprises two main types: chip-scale OEOs and non-chip-scale OEOs. Chip-scale OEOs are compact devices integrating optical and electronic components on a single chip, offering enhanced performance and versatility for applications like telecommunications and signal processing. In contrast, non-chip-scale OEOs typically involve larger, more complex setups, often providing higher output power and broader bandwidth. Both types cater to demands in high-frequency signal generation, with varying advantages in size, efficiency, and application scope.</p></p>
