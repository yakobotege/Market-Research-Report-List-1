<p><p>The traditional alkaline electrolyser market is primarily segmented into two types based on the electrolyte used: Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) and Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH). KOH is favored for its higher ionic conductivity, enhancing efficiency in hydrogen production. Conversely, NaOH is often more cost-effective, making it popular in budget-sensitive applications. Both electrolytes enable the electrolysis process in alkaline conditions, facilitating hydrogen generation, with specific advantages influencing their adoption across various industrial and energy sectors, driving growth in the market.</p></p>
