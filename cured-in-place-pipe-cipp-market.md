<p><p>Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) is a trenchless rehabilitation method used to repair existing pipelines without the need for extensive excavation. CIPP offers a cost-effective solution for maintaining infrastructure, minimizing disruption, and extending the lifespan of pipes.</p><p>The CIPP market is witnessing significant growth driven by increasing investments in infrastructure development, a rising focus on sustainable practices, and the need to upgrade aging water and wastewater systems. <p><p>Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) is a trenchless rehabilitation technique used to repair existing pipelines. The main market types include Inversion Type and Pull-in Type. Inversion Type involves inverting a resin-impregnated felt tube inside the damaged pipe, allowing the material to conform to the pipe's shape as it cures. Meanwhile, Pull-in Type uses a flexible liner that is pulled through the existing pipe, allowing for rapid installation and minimizing disruptions. Both methods enhance durability and extend the life of aging infrastructure.</p></p>
