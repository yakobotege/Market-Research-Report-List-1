<p><p>The vitreous tamponades market divides into two primary types: gaseous and liquid tamponades. Gaseous tamponades involve the use of gases like sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) or perfluoropropane (C3F8) that expand within the eye, providing support for retinal detachment or surgical recovery. Liquid tamponades, on the other hand, utilize silicone oil or heavy liquids, offering sustained support and visibility during surgeries. Both types serve essential roles in ocular procedures, depending on the clinical situation and desired outcomes.</p></p>
