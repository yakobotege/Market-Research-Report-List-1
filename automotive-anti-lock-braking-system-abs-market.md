<p><p>The Automotive Anti-Lock Braking System (ABS) market features two primary types: Pneumatic ABS and Hydraulic ABS. Pneumatic ABS utilizes air pressure to modulate brake forces, commonly found in commercial vehicles. In contrast, Hydraulic ABS employs hydraulic fluid to regulate braking pressure, providing superior control and responsiveness in passenger vehicles. Both systems enhance vehicle safety by preventing wheel lock-up during sudden braking, improving traction and stability on various road conditions, thereby reducing the risk of accidents.</p></p>
