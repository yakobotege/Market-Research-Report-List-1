<p><p>Industrial 5G networks are categorized into two main types based on frequency spectrum: Sub-6 GHz and mmWave. Sub-6 GHz networks provide wider coverage and better penetration through obstacles, making them suitable for various industrial applications where mobility and consistency are crucial. In contrast, mmWave networks offer significantly higher data rates and lower latency over short distances, ideal for dense environments or applications requiring real-time data processing, such as automation and augmented reality in manufacturing and logistics.</p></p>
